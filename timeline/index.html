  1. Birth of Samuel Hepburn

    1698

    James H. Campbell’s great-grandfather was born in Glasgow, Scotland. His descendants later settled in America to escape religious persecution.

  2. Marriage of Samuel Hepburn and Janet Sinclair

    1746

    Samuel Hepburn married Janet Sinclair in Scotland before the family relocated to Ireland and eventually America.

  3. Hepburn Family Emigrates to America

    1773

    James Hepburn, Campbell’s grandfather, and his brother William sailed from Ireland to Philadelphia to pursue new opportunities.

  4. Marriage of James Hepburn and Mary Hopewell

    1781

    Campbell’s grandparents wed, and their union produced nine children, including Jane H. Campbell, his mother.

  5. Birth of Francis C. Campbell

    1787

    James H. Campbell’s father was born in York, Pennsylvania, and later became a prominent lawyer.

  6. Era of Good Feelings Begins

    1816

    The period of national unity under President James Monroe coincided with the marriage of Francis C. Campbell and Jane H. Hepburn.

  7. First Public School in Philadelphia

    1818

    Philadelphia established its first public education institution, reflecting broader educational reforms.

  8. Birth of James Hepburn Campbell and Historical Context

    February 8, 1820

    Born in Williamsport, PA, to Francis C. and Jane Campbell. His birth coincided with the Missouri Compromise, a yellow fever outbreak in Philadelphia, the construction of Fairmount Waterworks, and the birth of Elvina Cadwell Williamson.

  9. Infrastructure and Transportation Advances

    1824

    The Pennsylvania Railroad Company was formed and the Erie Canal opened, revolutionizing trade. Also, Courtland Howell’s father passed away, marking a turning point in his early life.

  10. Legal Education and Career Beginnings

    1840-1841

    Campbell enrolled in Dickinson Law School as the Second Great Awakening ended. He graduated in August 1841, was admitted to the bar on October 18, and began his law practice in Pottsville, PA.

  11. Marriage and Family

    June 1843

    He married Juliet Hamersley Lewis, daughter of Pennsylvania Chief Justice Ellis Lewis. The couple had four children, though two were lost during the Civil War.

  12. Political Awakening

    1844

    Campbell attended the Whig National Convention supporting Henry Clay, amid anti-immigration riots in Philadelphia targeting Irish Catholic communities.

  13. Mexican-American War

    1846-1848

    The U.S. expanded territorially during the Mexican-American War, acquiring significant portions of the Southwest.

  14. Seneca Falls Convention

    1848

    A landmark event for women’s rights, the Seneca Falls Convention marked a turning point in the fight for gender equality.

  15. Gold Rush and Cholera Outbreak

    1849

    The California Gold Rush spurred mass migration to the West, while a cholera outbreak in Philadelphia highlighted public health challenges. Meanwhile, Courtland Howell transitioned from railroads to banking.

  16. Fugitive Slave Act Passed

    1850

    The controversial Fugitive Slave Act intensified national tensions over slavery.

  17. Congressional Career

    1855-1857, 1859-1863

    Campbell was elected as the representative of Pennsylvania’s 11th Congressional District in the 34th, 36th, and 37th Congresses, championing anti-slavery and protective tariff policies, and sponsoring the Pacific Railroad Act.

  18. Political Advocacy

    1860

    He delivered a pivotal speech on the Morrill Tariff and served on the Committee of Thirty-Three, opposing compromises on slavery as national tensions escalated.

  19. Civil War Service

    1861-1863

    Campbell enlisted in Cassius Clay’s Washington Guards within two weeks of the first shots at Sumter. He served as a Major of the 25th PA Volunteers, interviewed President Lincoln and his wife in early 1862, and raised the 39th Regiment in 1863 to counter Lee’s invasion.

  20. Diplomatic Service in Sweden

    1864-1867

    Appointed Minister Resident to Sweden on May 18, Campbell strengthened U.S.-Sweden relations and helped thwart Confederate plots in Europe.

  21. National Tragedies and Honors

    1865

    The assassination of Lincoln and the ratification of the 13th Amendment marked a tumultuous year. Campbell was also honored as a President of Honor in the Institut d’Afrique in Paris on February 17.

  22. Later Life

    1867-1895

    Returning to Philadelphia, Campbell resumed his law practice and was elected a Companion of the Military Order of the Loyal Legion. The Howell family also relocated during this period.

  23. Economic and Cultural Shifts

    1873

    The Panic of 1873 triggered an economic depression, even as cultural milestones like the opening of the Philadelphia Zoo—the first in the U.S.—reshaped society.

  24. Centennial Exposition

    1876

    Philadelphia hosted the World’s Fair, celebrating U.S. innovation and progress, coinciding with Campbell's continued influence in legal circles.

  25. Legal Landmarks

    1883

    Campbell argued the landmark Packer Estate Case before the Pennsylvania Supreme Court, and standard time zones were established to streamline railroad schedules.

  26. 50th Wedding Anniversary

    1893

    Campbell celebrated his 50th wedding anniversary at the Aeola estate in Wayne, PA, marking half a century of marriage.

  27. Death of James Hepburn Campbell

    1895

    Campbell died of heart failure in 1895, his passing following the economic downturn of the Panic of 1893, marking the end of an influential era in law and politics.

  28. Death of Courtland David Howell

    1890s

    Courtland David Howell passed away in the 1890s, concluding a life shaped by industrial and social change.
